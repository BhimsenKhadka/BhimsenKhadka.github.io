Current Research

My current research develops stochastic control models for subscription and cancellation decisions under positive mean-reverting utility processes. I study Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman variational inequalities, optimal switching problems, and numerical finite-difference methods with applications to subscription economics and mathematical finance.

About

I am a Ph.D. Scholar in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at The University of Texas at Dallas. My current research studies optimal subscription and cancellation decisions using stochastic control and Hamilton--Jacobi--Bellman variational inequalities. In particular, I am interested in positive mean-reverting utility processes, two-threshold switching policies, and numerical finite-difference methods for free-boundary problems.
