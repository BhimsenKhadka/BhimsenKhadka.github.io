Research

My research focuses on stochastic control, Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman equations, optimal switching, mean-reverting stochastic processes, free-boundary problems, and applications to subscription economics and mathematical finance.

Stochastic Control and Subscription Models

Developing stochastic control models for optimal subscription and cancellation decisions under uncertainty and consumer heterogeneity.

Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman Equations

Studying HJB variational inequalities, value matching, smooth pasting, and optimal threshold policies arising in stochastic optimization.

Mean-Reverting Processes and Free-Boundary Problems

Analyzing optimal switching problems driven by positive mean-reverting utility processes and solving the associated free-boundary problems.

Computational Methods

Developing finite-difference algorithms and numerical techniques for stochastic control and mathematical finance.
