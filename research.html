Research

My research focuses on stochastic control, optimal switching, HJB equations, and mean-reverting utility models.

Positive Mean-Reverting Subscription Model

I study subscription decisions where consumer utility follows a positive mean-reverting diffusion. This framework captures both positivity and the tendency of satisfaction to return toward a long-run average level.

Optimal Switching and HJB Variational Inequalities

The model leads to coupled HJB variational inequalities, threshold policies, value-matching conditions, and smooth-pasting conditions.

Numerical Free-Boundary Problems

Since the characteristic polynomial method fails under positive mean reversion, finite difference methods are used to compute value functions and optimal thresholds.

Mathematical Finance and Subscription Economics

I am interested in applications of stochastic control to economic decision-making, pricing, churn, and subscription management.
